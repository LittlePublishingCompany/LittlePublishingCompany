Sun. Moon. Day.
Three.

Sun. Moon. Day.
Two.
 
            The room is full. Lonely. Death is not a companion, but divorce.
 
Sun. Moon. Day.
One.
 
            Broke my leg once. Thought I would die. Cute nurse. Married her beneath sycamores on a Tuesday. 
 
Sun. Moon.
 
 
Antiquity’s Tether
 
 
            He’s with me but he’s not. Distance in the eyes.
            Einstein demanded relativity. Einstein was wrong. Time isn’t measured in gravity and seconds. It’s measured by the seconds you want back.
             Deep grey, distant eyes—Antiquity’s Tether. I see us crawling up from the primordial filth. He walks away.
 
 
Sometimes It’s All I Can Say
 
 
            Bills neatly arranged tall as the Alico. Eight messages, “We need to talk.”
            I step into grey morning. Wind cuts my triceps. My cigarette quivers, falls. I let it roll there. “Is this it?”
            The sun ruptures through, warming amber sky. Exhale. “Fuck it, it’s gonna be a good day.”
